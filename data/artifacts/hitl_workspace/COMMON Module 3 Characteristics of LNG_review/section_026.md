## Liquefied Natural Gas (LNG) Properties

Natural gas produced from the wellhead consists of methane, ethane, propane, and heavier hydrocarbons, plus small quantities of nitrogen, helium, carbon dioxide, sulfur compounds, and water. LNG is liquefied natural gas .



**[Figure 33: The image displays a learning objective labeled "Learning Objective 5," which instructs the reader to describe the key definitions of the characteristics of liquefied natural gas (LNG), emphasizing the need for an accurate and comprehensive understanding of its properties and attributes.]**



The LNG Liquefaction process first requires pre-treatment of the natural gas stream to remove impurities such as mercury, water, carbon dioxide, hydrogen sulfide, and other sulfur compounds. By removing these impurities, solids cannot be formed while the gas is refrigerated.  The final product will then meet the quality specifications of the LNG end users.

Natural gas is liquefied at high pressure and warmer temperature than -256 o F.  Then, its pressure is reduced to be stored slightly above atmospheric pressure and below -256 o F.  LNG takes up only 1/600th of the volume required for a comparable amount of natural gas at room temperature and normal atmospheric pressure.  Because it is not stored under pressure, the common misperception of LNG as a pressurized substance has led to an erroneous understanding of its danger.

LNG is a clear, non-corrosive, non-toxic, near odorless, cryogenic liquid at normal atmospheric pressure.  Although methane is non-toxic, as with any gaseous material besides air and oxygen, natural gas that is vaporized from LNG can cause asphyxiation due to lack of oxygen if a concentration of gas develops in an unventilated, confined area.
