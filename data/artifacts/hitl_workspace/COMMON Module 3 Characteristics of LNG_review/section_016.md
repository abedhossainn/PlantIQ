## Physical Properties of Hydrocarbons

The properties of each type of hydrocarbon depend on the number of carbon and hydrogen atoms in the hydrocarbon molecules, and how they are joined.

Hydrocarbon is the name given to the large family of chemical compounds made up entirely of hydrogen and carbon.  Crude oil is a mixture composed almost entirely of hydrocarbons, with a few other compounds like hydrogen sulfide (H2S) present in some cases.
