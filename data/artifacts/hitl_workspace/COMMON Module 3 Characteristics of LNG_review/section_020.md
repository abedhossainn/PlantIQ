## States of Matter

All matter exists in one of three states: solid, liquid, or a gas (refer to Figure 4 ).

1. In a solid , molecules are close together and are slow-moving.
2. In a liquid , molecules are further apart and move faster than in a solid.
3. In a gas , molecules are far apart and move very rapidly.

The rate of molecule movement determines whether matter is a solid, a liquid, or a gas.

- Solids do not need a container as they have a definite shape and volume of their own.
- Liquids have a definite volume, but no definite shape. Liquids take the shape of whatever container they are put in.
- Gases have no definite shape or volume and expand to fill a container, no matter what its size or shape.  A gas not enclosed in a container keeps on expanding until its molecules become so far apart, it is impossible to detect the gas.
