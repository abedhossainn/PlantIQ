## Glossary of Terms

The glossary provides definitions of major terms used in this module. Familiarity with these terms will assist you in performing your job effectively.

|TERM|DEFINITION|
|--------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
|CFM|Cubic Feet per Minute|
|Cryogenic|In Physics, Cryogenics is the study of the production and behavior of materials at extremely low temperatures (below - 292.00°F).|
|Emergency|An event that disrupts normal operating conditions and can pose a threat to human life, health, property, or the environment, if not immediately controlled.|
|Hydrocarbons|Substance made of hydrogen and carbon (among other elements). Common hydrocarbons found in Natural Gas (AKA 'Fuel Gas' @CovePoint LNG): • Methane • Ethane • Propane • Butane • Pentane|
|LNG|Liquefied Natural Gas: Natural gas composed primarily of methane that is cooled to a temperature of about - 260°F at atmospheric pressure where it condenses to a liquid.|
|LPG|Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG), called simply ' propane ' or ' butane, ' are flammable mixtures of hydrocarbon gases stored in pressurized containers.|
|MSDS/SDS|Material Safety Data Sheet / Safety Data Sheet|
|psi|Pounds per Square Inch|
|psig|Pounds per Square Inch (Gauge)|
