## MODULE 3 OVERVIEW

This self-study module is designed to equip Operations Field Technicians with the knowledge and familiarization of the properties associated with LNG, how to be prepared to recognize process hazards, how LNG can produce cryogenic burns, how to handle cryogenic liquids, and how to wear the proper personal protective equipment (PPE).

The possibility of large spills of liquefied natural gas (LNG) from large, insulated storage tanks or ocean-going LNG tank ships has received increased attention as the demand for natural gas has increased.  This attention has resulted because:

- LNG has hazardous properties,
- LNG is stored at extremely low temperatures in large, insulated storage tanks, and
- LNG is transported in large, highly visible ships.

LNG spills are considered serious events because the spill surface provides a large heat source that will rapidly vaporize the LNG. The resultant flammable vapor cloud can travel downwind before diluting to safe concentrations. If the flammable vapor is ignited, a vapor cloud fire followed by a pool fire can occur.

Learning objectives are provided to guide you to the major points in the material and to help you gauge your understanding of the content and your progress.  To obtain maximum benefit, each person completing this self-study module should study the material covered and answer the review questions when finished.  Each question is designed to address the listed associated learning objectives.
