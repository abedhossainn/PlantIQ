## Rollover

When LNG supplies of multiple densities (various places of origin, age, etc.) are loaded into a tank one at a time, they do not initially mix.  Instead, they will layer themselves in unstable strata within the tank.  As the lower LNG layer is heated by normal heat leak, it changes density until it finally becomes lighter than the upper layer.  At that point, a liquid rollover will occur, which can cause a sudden increase in vapor generation within the tank.  It is possible to experience a rollover so violent as to generate more vapor than the tank's pressure control devices (PSV's, vents, boil -off gas compression) can handle, which can cause cracks or other structural failures in the tank.

To help prevent the layering, or stratification, the operators unloading an LNG vessel, or simply consolidating inventory, monitor the density of both the product being transferred and the product in the receiving tank, and decide whether to fill the receiving tank from the bottom or the top fill connections.
