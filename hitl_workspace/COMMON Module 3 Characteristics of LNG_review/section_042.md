## Vapor Clouds

As LNG leaves a temperature-controlled container, it begins to warm up, returning the liquid to a gaseous state.  Initially, the gas is colder and heavier than the surrounding air.  It creates a fog -a vapor cloud -above the released liquid.  As the gas warms, it mixes with the surrounding air and begins to disperse.  The vapor cloud will only ignite if it encounters an ignition source while concentrated within its flammability range.
