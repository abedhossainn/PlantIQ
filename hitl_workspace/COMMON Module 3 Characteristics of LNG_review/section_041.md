## Explosion

An explosion happens when a substance rapidly changes its chemical state, i.e., is ignited or is uncontrollably released from a pressurized state.  For an uncontrolled release to happen, there must be a structural failure (ruptured pipe, tank, pump, etc.). LNG tanks store the liquid at an extremely low temperature, about -256°F, so no pressure is required to maintain its liquid state.  Since LNG is stored at atmospheric pressure, a crack or puncture in the container will not create an immediate explosion.
