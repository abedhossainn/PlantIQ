## Combined Gas Law

Boyle's Law and Charles' Law refer to ' isothermal ' and ' isobaric ' changes, respectively. However, during many gas processes, the gas pressure and temperature change simultaneously. Therefore, it is desirable to use a formula that relates to the three variables of pressure, temperature, and volume.

In hydrocarbon processing, all gases are contained in pipelines and vessels.  This makes it possible to calculate the volume of gas in a vessel or system.  To remove the effects of temperature and pressure on the volume of the gas, all calculations are made assuming a standard temperature of 60°F and a pressure of 14.7 psia (air pressure at sea level).

The following properties of gases are described below.

- ▪

- Density per cubic foot at Standard Temperature and Pressure (STP) (60°F)

The density of a gas is mass per unit volume measured in pounds and 14.7 psi.  Even if the volume of a gas is measured under different conditions, it is not difficult to calculate its volume at STP, using the gas laws.

- Compressibility

This is the ability to be compressed in volume.  All gases can be compressed.  Compressing a gas reduces its volume by squeezing the gas molecules together, thereby making it hotter.

- Flammability

This is the ability to burn in air. Hydrocarbon gases are flammable.

- ▪

Inertness This is the property whereby a gas does not react chemically with other matter.  Nitrogen is an inert gas used to remove air or hydrocarbons from equipment.  An inert gas is not flammable under normal conditions.
