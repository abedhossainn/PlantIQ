## Fire Hazards Summary

Questions about LNG safety often demonstrate how LNG is often confused with other fuels and materials.  All these fuels can be used safely if proper safety, security, and environmental protections are in place.  We fill our cars and trucks with gasoline, use LPG (propane) in our backyard grills, and use methane to heat our homes, hundreds of millions of times each day, and serious safety incidents are rare.

In summary, LNG is an extremely cold, non-toxic, non-corrosive substance that is transferred and stored at atmospheric pressure.  It is refrigerated, rather than pressurized, which enables LNG to be an effective, economical method of transporting large volumes of natural gas over long distances. LNG poses little danger if it is contained in storage tanks, piping, and equipment designed for use in LNG cryogenic conditions.  However, vapors resulting from LNG because of an uncontrolled release can be hazardous, within the key properties of LNG and LNG vapors -flammability range and in contact with a source of ignition -as described above.
